Your Ghanaian Journey Begins Here

Why Visit Ghana?

From the poignant history of the Cape Coast and Elmina Castles to the bustling markets of Accra and Kumasi, the lush rainforests of Kakum, and the wildlife of Mole National Park, Ghana offers something for every traveler. And don't forget our warm hospitality and incredible cuisine!

When to Visit

Dry Season (November to March): Sunny skies and lower humidity, ideal for exploring most of the country.

Rainy Season (April to October): Expect more frequent rainfall, especially in the southern regions. However, the landscapes are lush and green. The heaviest rains are typically from June to September. Here in Accra, you'll likely experience the main rainy season from around May to July, with a shorter, less intense rainy period in September/October.

Visa Information

Visa requirements vary depending on your nationality. Please check the official website of the Ghana Immigration Service well in advance of your trip to understand the specific requirements for your passport.

Health
& Safety

Consult your doctor about recommended vaccinations and malaria precautions. It's advisable to drink bottled or treated water. Ghana is generally a safe country, but it's always wise to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft, especially in crowded areas.

Practical Tips for Your Trip

Getting Around Accra & Ghana

In Accra, you can use taxis, ride-sharing apps like Uber and Bolt, and the local "tro-tros" (minibuses) for a more adventurous experience. For travel between cities, consider domestic flights or comfortable long-distance buses.

Accommodation

Accra offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses in areas like Osu and Labadi to international hotels in Airport Residential Area and Cantonments. Consider your budget and desired location when booking.

Money Matters

The local currency is the Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). You can exchange foreign currency at Kotoka International Airport or at forex bureaus in major cities like Accra. Credit and debit cards are accepted in some larger hotels and restaurants, but it's wise to carry some cash, especially for smaller establishments and markets. Tipping is appreciated in restaurants and for good service.

Staying Connected

You can purchase a local SIM card with data upon arrival at the airport or from mobile network shops in Accra. Wi-Fi is available in many hotels and some cafes.

Electricity

The standard voltage in Ghana is 230V, and the frequency is 50Hz. The plugs are typically Type G (British three-pin). You may need a plug adapter.
